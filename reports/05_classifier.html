Metagenre Classification - Genre Discourse Analysis

Author

Markus Radke

Published

November 28, 2025

This report presents the evaluation results of different classifier models trained to predict the MusicBrainz folksonomy meta-genres. Classifiers were trained for both low detailed (10-15 genres) and high detailed (25-30) meta-genre taxonomies.

The metagenre POPULAR MUSIC was discarded for analysis as the metagenre was only present for too few tracks.

1 Complete Workflow

Caution

Currently, the models are tranined with a fixed undersampling rate with factor 10 (low-detail) and 20 (high-detail) due to computational constraints. Also, the oversampling rate is fixed to 0.25 for both low-detail and high-detailed metagenre labels.

Note

The mixture of lasso and ridge regularization for the GLMNET models was fixed to 0.5 (elastic net).

Random forests were tuned with 750 trees per ensemble. The final model on the whole tranining set was trained with 1000 trees.

Note

Variables starting with “dtb.” refer to artist genres from Spotify. We assume that artist genres are assigned with a strategic aim for distribution on the platform (Valverde, 2025). In that regard, they are different from the musical genres assigned by the listeners. The variables are flags indicating whether an artist genre is assigned to a track’s artist or not. To reduce the dimensionality, we used the same hiearchical-tree-based approach as for the MusicBrainz meta-genres.
